Vada Pav

About

Often called the "Indian burger," Vada Pav is Mumbai’s most iconic street food — simple, spicy, and deeply satisfying. It features a golden, deep-fried potato fritter (vada) tucked into a soft bun (pav), typically slathered with spicy green chutney, tangy tamarind sauce, and a sprinkle of dry garlic chutney for that fiery punch. What makes Vada Pav truly special is its universal appeal — from office-goers to college students, street vendors to stockbrokers, it’s a snack that unites Mumbai. Whether you're rich or poor, Vada Pav is a humble favorite that transcends class, offering bold flavors and comforting familiarity in every single bite.

Steps

  1. Boil and mash 2 large potatoes (about 350g); set aside.
  2. Crush 6–7 garlic cloves and 1–2 green chilies.
  3. Heat 2–3 tsp oil, crackle ½ tsp mustard seeds, add 7–8 curry leaves, a pinch of asafoetida, and ⅛ tsp turmeric powder.
  4. Sauté crushed garlic-chili mix briefly until fragrant.
  5. Pour this tempering into mashed potatoes; mix with 1–2 tbsp chopped coriander and salt to taste.
  6. Shape into small round vadas and flatten slightly.
  7. Make a batter with 1–1.25 cups gram flour (besan), ⅛ tsp turmeric, a pinch of hing, salt, and enough water for a thick flowing consistency.
  8. Dip each vada into the batter and deep-fry until crisp and golden.
  9. Slice pav from your favorite bakery, spread green and/or sweet chutney inside.
  10. Place one hot vada in each pav, optionally sprinkle dry garlic chutney.
  11. Serve hot with fried salted green chilies and a cup of chai!!
